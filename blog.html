  4. Mines Undergraduate Research Fellowship

    This post signifies the successful culmination of my year-long undergraduate research opportunity under the guidance of my graduate mentor, Hoon Seo. Throughout this enriching journey, I immersed myself in the fascinating world of neural networks and machine learning, gaining invaluable hands-on experience.


    The research focused on transforming drilling data from a real-world company into a comprehensive 3-dimensional model of underground ore deposits. Our approach involved implementing a Generative Adversarial Imputation Network (GAIN) algorithm. This sophisticated model filled gaps in the data with estimations of true values, and subsequently conducted testing to verify the accuracy of the generated data. If the process detected a distinct difference between actual values and estimations, the model was refined and improved until there were no differences discernable.


    I am immensely grateful for the opportunity provided by the Mines Undergraduate Research Fellowship, which played a significant role in shaping my learning journey. The knowledge and skills acquired during this research endeavor will undoubtedly influence my future computer science applications and projects.
